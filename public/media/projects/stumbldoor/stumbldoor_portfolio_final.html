The Challenge

A Library in Crisis

From 2009 to 2022, physical library visits declined by 21.2%. Yet library card registrations peaked in 2019. This paradox reveals a critical gap: people want to read, but the traditional library experience is failing them.

The Problem

Libraries face an identity crisis. While younger generations still value physical books, they're intimidated by formal library spaces, struggle to navigate complex layouts, and crave a more personalized, social reading experience.

  • 21.2% decrease in physical visits (2009-2022)
  • Library card registrations peaked in 2019
  • Fostering next-generation library-goers is critical

Our Research

We conducted comprehensive research across 4 NYC libraries, surveying 35 users and conducting 8 in-depth interviews. Our findings revealed three critical barriers: intimidating spaces, confusing navigation, and lack of personalization.

  • 35 survey responses
  • 8 in-depth interviews
  • 4 library field studies
User Research

Meet Our Users

Through interviews and observations, we developed three key personas that guided our design. Each represents a distinct user need and motivation.

Nerdy Nancy

22, Undergraduate

"I appreciate when librarians can help me navigate those overwhelming research databases."

Nancy needs guidance through complex information systems and values expert support.

Lazy Lucy

23, Graduate

"I feel more comfortable studying at home. I can rest when I want to and play when I feel like it."

Lucy values flexibility and comfort, preferring a personalized experience over rigid library rules.

Techie Tony

26, Graduate

"I prefer an environment where I am aware of other people working around me. I find it motivating."

Tony seeks community and social motivation, thriving in collaborative environments.

Key Insight

All three personas revealed a common thread: users want the resources of a library, but with the comfort and convenience of a personalized, digital experience. They crave guidance, flexibility, and community—not isolation.

The Solution

Stumbldoor: Three Pillars of Innovation

Stumbldoor transforms the library experience into an interactive adventure. It's not just a tool; it's a gateway to a world of knowledge and community, seamlessly integrating the digital and physical.

01
Scrollstack
Hyper-personalized reading playlists, like Spotify for books. Our algorithm analyzes your reading history to deliver curated recommendations. This solves the "what to read next" problem that plagues modern readers.
Design Insight:
  • Addresses the discovery problem identified in user research
  • Personalizes the library experience for each user
  • Reduces decision paralysis when choosing books
02
Gameful UX
Achievements, reading stats, and community leaderboards turn reading into a rewarding game. This feature directly addresses Techie Tony's need for motivation and community, making reading a social and joyful activity.
Design Insight:
  • Transforms reading from solitary to social
  • Provides recognition and achievement
  • Increases engagement through gamification
03
AR Assist
An augmented reality navigator for library layouts. Simply point your phone to find the exact location of a book. This solves the navigation nightmare identified in our research, transforming a frustrating search into a magical experience.
Design Insight:
  • Eliminates the confusion of navigating large libraries
  • Reduces time spent searching for books
  • Makes the physical library visit more enjoyable
Design Process

From Insight to Interface

Our design process was iterative and user-centric, moving from research to brainstorming to prototyping to visual design.

Research & Synthesis

We started with affinity mapping to synthesize our research findings. We identified key user pain points and opportunities, then brainstormed solutions focused on three core themes: gamifying knowledge discovery, integrating online-offline experiences, and creating a digital silent zone.

Prototyping & Testing

We created low-fidelity wireframes to test core functionality, then moved to high-fidelity prototypes for user testing. Our information architecture was designed to be intuitive, with clear user flows that make finding and borrowing books effortless.

Brand & Visual Design

We developed a brand voice that is reliable, comfy, and intelligent. Our tagline—"Curiosity Stumbls, Knowledge Unfolds"—captures the spirit of discovery. The visual design is warm, approachable, and modern, reflecting the brand values.

Final Visuals

The final app design showcases a clean, intuitive interface that makes the library experience feel less intimidating and more inviting. Every design decision was grounded in our user research and brand values.
